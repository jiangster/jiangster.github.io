Random Music Generator

App Design

About

RMG - Random Music Generator - is an app developed by Xingbo Wang that procedurally generates tunes. It allows users to easily finetune musical properties and export to MIDI.

In this project I worked closely with Xingbo as a UX/UI designer to translate the functioning prototype into a cohesive app design. My workflow consisted of:
1. Breaking down the user stories, flow, and task analyses
2. Creating sitemaps, wireframes, and high fidelity mockups
3. Conducting usability tests to validate design
4. Developing a cohesive visual language (branding TBD)



Process

RMG is used primarily for three functions: customizing, saving, listening to tunes. I sketched out various userflows and sitemaps, connecting relevant interactions and simplifying each step across for every iteration. For example: customization and randomization are like the chicken and the egg — either function can be initiated first in this case, thus demanding a looped interaction in the design. The challenge was how to design the app to seamlessly integrates the two functions.

The userflow was simplified to the version below. I created a set of monochromatic wireframes to focus on the usability and effectiveness of the experience.



A/B Testing

Gestures and animations were taken into consideration during the design process. For example, when users tap "Customize," the intention is for the playback screen to minimize and float either up (Version A) or down (Version B). Though it initially seemed counterintuitive to animate the playback from the bottom of the screen to the top, users responded more positively to Version A because it became more comfortable to interact with the customization screen as it is closer to where fingers can reach.

Visual Language

RMG generates unique tunes, and so one challenge was giving each tune an identity. Each tune is visualized by a unique vector shape.

Simplicity was key to the design without sacrificing understanding and usability. I created clean, sleek screens that simplified interactions, tasks and ideas.

Building Stories

February - March 2015 / UX Design, Branding, Visual Design

Project Scope

I've always liked the idea refining existing designs. That's why I was eager to get down to business when the Heritage Resources Centre approached my team of 6 at the University of Waterloo for a redesign solution. We were presented with Building Stories, a user-generated database of historic Canadian sites and buildings. The problem? Both the web and mobile platform are extremely outdated and not user friendly, which discourages users from signing up and sharing the Canadian heritage. Our job was to create a new interface and brand identity to invite more users to Building Stories.

Throughout the project, I was responsible for leading the website redesign and creating a new branding scheme.





The Approach

We dedicated the first 3 weeks to user testing, stakeholder interviews and problem identification. We got up close and personal with both the website and app to summarize the biggest usability issues, including: an unintuitive navigation system, lack of visual heirarchy, and confusing content organization.

The Challenge

One of the coolest things about my campus in Stratford is this three-storey Christie MicroTile display (below). My three Digital Media classmates and I were challenged to create an interactive piece that effectively used the unorthodox screensize. After much planning, designing and testing we came up with an interactive game with a "green" initiative. We call our piece Space Junk.


The Game

Space Junk is a shooter-style game that aims to encourage youths to keep the Earth free of trash. We developed it for children in elementary and middle school. Players play as Tommy Terra - a boy concerned with the environment - to obliterate junk monsters falling from the sky with his magic balloons.

My Role

Throughout this project, I was responsible for the conceptualization of the game and graphic designs. After deciding that a vertical shooter-style game would most effectively use these dimensions, I sat down to brainstorm and sketch possible themes. The idea of Space Junk came from my determination to design with a purpose. I didn't want to create any shooting game; I wanted to create a game that can spread a positive message. What do we need to eliminate around us? With that question in mind, I found the idea of Space junk and set out to craft a small set of cute characters that would make this game appropriate for all ages and full of life.

The Leone Ranger

iOS Game

The Game

The Leone Ranger is an adventure platformer that teaches children in Sierra Leone about safe sanitation practices. It follows the imaginative journey of a young, curious boy named "Bo" as he encounters cute talking animals and mini challenges along his trek to school.

Where It Started

The ebola outbreak was at its peak in late 2014 and Sierra Leone was one of the three main affected regions. As a part of a 3-month mobile game design course at the University of Waterloo, my team and I took the challenge of designing a game to teach children about safe sanitation practices - an unique scenario which we tackled through creativity, cultural-awareness and empathy.

Over the next 3 months we conducted research about Sierra Leone and ebola, created storyboards, completed a business plan and showcased a working prototype.

Background Research

Any epidemic is a sensitive, heavy topic and we understood that tackling this ebola in an educational game is not an easy task. Thus we researched and researched the topic, understanding the people and culure of Sierra Leone and how they are being affected by the outbreak.

Our findings showed that the children were highly susceptible to the virus due to a few reasons:
1. Low quality or lack of health education at school
2. Lack of parental guidance on proper sanitation practices and preventative measures
3. Inability to access information about the virus and prevention
There exists a few games in the same market (see wash-united.org), however there is a lack of free games that directly address the specific effects of ebola.



Storyboard & Development

As our target users are young children from ages 6-12, we wanted to create a game that is lighthearted, colorful and exploratory. As a result we went with an adventure platformer that allows the players to freely interact with characters and the environment. Each segment of exploration was paired with a mini challenge including: "Droplet collection", "Water attack", and a mini quiz.

Throughout the development of our game, I was responsible for the ux and visual design. I designed the game art to accurately portray the style, culture, and environment of Sierra Leone, with all sprites crafted in a pixel art style.
